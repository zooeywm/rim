## What this module does

The notification center is the durable inbox for runtime feedback:

- command failures and validation messages land here
- opening the center marks the selected item as read
- you can keep history visible even after transient toast-style previews disappear

<Aside type="note" title="Default entry points">
	Use `:notifications`, `:noti`, or the default normal-mode binding `<leader>n` to open the center.
</Aside>

## Generate a visible notification first

<Steps>
	1. Open the command palette with `:`.
	2. Type a command you know is invalid, such as `definitely-not-a-command`.
	3. Press `Enter`.
	4. Observe the immediate error feedback.
	5. Open the notification center using `:notifications`.
</Steps>

## Move through items

<Steps>
	1. Confirm the center is open.
	2. Use `Down` or `j` to move to newer or older items depending on the current selection.
	3. Use `Up` or `k` to move back.
	4. Watch unread items become read as they become selected.
	5. Press `Esc` when you want to close the center without deleting anything.
</Steps>

## Manage the queue

<Tabs syncKey="notification-center-actions">
	<TabItem label="Open">
		Use `:notifications`, `:noti`, or `<leader>n`.
	</TabItem>
	<TabItem label="Move">
		Use `Up` and `Down`, or `k` and `j`, to change the selected item.
	</TabItem>
	<TabItem label="Delete">
		Use `d` to delete the selected notification from the stored list.
	</TabItem>
	<TabItem label="Help">
		Use `F1` to inspect the notification-center-specific keymap while the overlay is open.
	</TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Practical cleanup drill

<Steps>
	1. Create two or three notifications by running a mix of valid and invalid commands.
	2. Open the notification center.
	3. Move through the list until all unread items are reviewed.
	4. Press `d` on one stale item you no longer need.
	5. Press `Esc` to return to the editor.
</Steps>

## Important behavior to remember

- If there are no notifications, the center still opens with a `No notifications` placeholder row.
- Opening the center closes other overlays first, so it becomes the active interaction surface.
- The notification center is for review and deletion, not for replaying commands.
